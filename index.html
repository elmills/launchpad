The rules that apply to every path
Never cash out a 401(k) when leaving. You'd lose 30–40% to taxes + penalty immediately. Roll it to an IRA — one phone call, costs nothing.
The Roth conversion ladder. Low/zero income year (school, home with kids)? Convert Traditional 401(k) money to Roth IRA. Pay tax at 10% or 0% — then it's tax-free forever.
HSA is the most portable account you own. Never expires. Never tied to an employer. After 65 it becomes a second IRA usable for anything. Max it every year you have access.

Optimal contribution order — fill the buckets in this sequence

The goal isn't to pick one account — it's to fill them in the right order to minimize taxes and maximize growth.

①
401(k) to get full match
Whatever % Nucor matches — capture every dollar of free money first. This is an immediate 50–100% return on that contribution.
②
Max the HSA ($4,300)
Triple tax advantage — the only account with tax-free contributions, growth, AND withdrawals. Use Nucor's free clinic. Never spend HSA money if you can help it.
③
Max rest of 401(k) ($23,500)
After match + HSA, fill the 401(k) to the $23,500 limit. Every dollar reduces taxable income today and compounds tax-deferred.
④
Roth IRA ($7,000)
Post-tax now, but 100% tax-free forever. Also your most flexible account — contributions (not earnings) can be withdrawn anytime without penalty if life changes.
⑤
Taxable brokerage
If you've maxed everything above and still have money to invest — open a taxable brokerage at Fidelity or Vanguard. No limits, but no tax shelter.
